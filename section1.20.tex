\leavevmode{\indent}The~\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riemann_curvature_tensor}{\emph{Riemann curvature tensor}
or \emph{Riemann\hbox{--}Christoffel tensor}}
(after \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernhard_Riemann}{\textbold{Bernhard Riemann}}
and \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elwin_Bruno_Christoffel}{\textbold{Elwin Bruno Christoffel}})
is the most common method
used to express
the curvature of Riemannian manifolds.
It’s a~tensor field,
it assigns a~tensor
to each point of a~Riemannian manifold,
that measures
the extent
to which the~metric tensor
is not locally isometric
to that of \inquotes{flat} space.
The curvature tensor
measures
noncommutativity
of the covariant derivative,
and as such is
the~integrability obstruction
for the~existænce
of an~isometry
with \inquotes{flat} space.
